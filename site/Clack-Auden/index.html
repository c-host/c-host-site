Clack Auden

Clack Auden researches and produces creative representations of and interventions into audience interactions. Areas of research include the history of the claque, crowds for hire, verbatim theater, and audio scores composed for prompting audience interactions and performing infrastructural work.

[site]

CLACK.LIFE explores the nature of audiencing—the ways crowds and individuals receive, react to, and shape media and events. The project examines how audiences have historically been assembled, influenced, and instrumentalized, highlighting the often-blurred lines between genuine and orchestrated group behavior. Through research and collected examples, it aims to reveal, challenge, and repurpose the mechanisms of audience formation and action.

[article] [exhibition site]

Between May and June 2020, amid demonstrations and uprisings across the U.S., a subset of the New Models community held a focused discussion around the mechanics and aesthetics of contemporary crowds.

This discussion was archived and contextualized by an evolving diagram of crowd dynamics and the research discussed as part of a special report with New Modles titles "Uncanny Rally". The diagram was exhibited at the Ujazdowski Castle Centre for Contemporary Art in Warsaw, Poland as part of exhibition "The Influencing Machine", and curated by Aaron Moulton.
